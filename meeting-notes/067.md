# Ream Meeting #67 Recap

## Overview

This week's meeting covered progress across several active workstreams, including leanMultisig benchmarking, Hive interoperability testing, and devnet-4 upgrades. The team has made meaningful strides in getting multiple clients to finalize against one another and in establishing a foundation for automated Hive testing within the official Ethereum Hive GitHub repository. A new participant also joined the call expressing interest in contributing to P2P work, and the team discussed the current state of P2P research and the preference to continue with libp2p until a concrete reason to explore alternatives arises.

## Development Progress

### leanMultisig Benchmarking & Research

- **Aggregation Benchmark**: Unnawut completed a benchmark analyzing how many aggregations can fit into a slot, exploring topologies to reach up to 10,000 attestations per slot.
- **WHIR Study Group Presentation**: Unnawut has the content nearly finalized and is incorporating feedback from Thomas and Emile. The presentation is expected to be delivered next week.

### Hive Interoperability Testing

- **Hive Access**: The team gained permission to contribute to the main Ethereum Hive GitHub repository, a significant milestone for interop testing.
- **RPC Compatibility Tests**: Derek added all main RPC compatibility tests and integrated the leanSpec client as a reference to ensure post-genesis tests are as true to source as possible. The 32 tests now run in approximately 8 minutes after Docker builds.
- **Client Inconsistencies Found**: Derek identified two inconsistencies — Lantern has a byte prefix issue, and Zeam reports its health endpoint under a different API name. A potential third issue involving ethlambda and Zeam failing finalization/fork choice alignment is under investigation.
- **Finalization Tests**: Kayden is working on adding finalization tests to Hive, unblocked now that Derek's scaffolding is in place.

### devnet-4 Upgrades & Test Vectors

- **leanMultisig devnet-4 Upgrade**: Shariq worked on upgrading the leanSig repo to devnet-4, resolving issues around files not being automatically included in Docker images. Validator and other config changes were addressed, and Zeam, Ream, and Grandine were successfully finalized against each other.
- **Test Vector Upgrade**: Kayden is upgrading test vectors to devnet-4, targeting completion this week.

### P2P

- **New Participant Interest**: Jeff joined the call as a new participant and expressed interest in contributing to P2P. The team noted that ethp2p is still in early research and that libp2p remains the current direction unless a concrete constraint is identified.
