# Ream Meeting #65 Recap

## Overview

This week's developer call had a smaller attendance than usual, with several team members still participating at EthCC[8]. The discussion covered ongoing sync and networking fixes, devnet metrics observations, and key takeaways from the conference's consensus sessions. Notable topics included a proposal to experiment with [Goldfish](https://ethresear.ch/t/unblocking-faster-finality-with-decoupled-consensus/24527) as an alternative fork-choice rule for future devnets, interest in exploring [ethp2p](https://github.com/ethp2p/ethp2p/) as a potential replacement for libP2P, expanding the client compatibility matrix into shared infrastructure, and a recent Google paper on post-quantum cryptography suggesting Q-Day could arrive as early as 2029. The team also discussed the importance of taking a stronger lead on both infrastructure and specification work going forward.

## Development Progress

### Forward Sync & Gossip

- **Sync protection (Derek)**: Submitted a PR to drop bad roots encountered during forward sync. Also identified and fixed a spec misalignment where the walk-back condition could overshoot the head on a fork and walk all the way back to Genesis — a problem especially for checkpoint-synced nodes.
- **GossipSub send queue (Derek)**: Investigating a send queue full issue with the GossipSub library. A fix is expected within the week.

### Devnet Metrics & Interop

- **Metrics gaps (Unnawut)**: Reviewed metrics collected during recent pqdevnet runs. Many metrics are missing or misnamed — unclear if the source is the client or the scripting. Signature aggregation per slot is not showing meaningful data. Will continue investigating metric by metric ahead of next pqdevnet interop.

### Devnet Infrastructure & Compatibility

- **Client compatibility matrix (Unnawut, Kolby)**: Currently the compatibility matrix exists in Ream but is only used internally. The proposal is to expand it into a shared, automated infrastructure — similar to Ethereum Panda Ops — so all teams can see a checklist of client readiness before starting a devnet.
- **Infra ownership (Shariq)**: Highlighted that devnet infrastructure is currently managed by a third party and that the team should take a stronger lead here, treating it as an accessible near-term win.

### Consensus Research: Goldfish & Finality

- **Goldfish proposal (Unnawut)**: At [FORT MODE](https://luma.com/fort_mode)'s roundtable session, Yann from the consensus team proposed that the pqdevnet team experiment with Goldfish's available chain. Goldfish replaces attestation subnets with a single committee (View Merge) and discards votes older than one slot (vote expiry / "the goldfish only remembers one slot back"). Three-slot finality is still the target. The team needs to assess how difficult it would be to replace the current fork-choice (LMD Ghost / 3SF) with Goldfish.
- **Finality gadget framing (Unnawut)**: The conference session clarified a three-chain model — justified chain (finality gadget), stable chain (high-confidence-but-not-final), and available chain — splitting what was previously bundled into a single chain.

### Networking: ethp2p Exploration

- **ethp2p prototype (Unnawut, Shariq)**: Raul's team demonstrated ethp2p with a trace visualization tool ([Netviz](https://netviz.ethp2p.dev/)) showing significantly faster propagation and far fewer duplicates compared to libp2p on the beacon chain. The proposal is to fork the client and prototype ethp2p for leanSig's larger signature sizes (~3 KB), and provide feedback to Raul on what works and what's missing. This is not a direct replacement for libp2p yet, so both would need to coexist.

### Key Separation & leanSig (Shariq)

- **devnet-4 key separation (Shariq)**: Completed separation of attester and proposer keys for devnet-4. Still needs to swap out the leanSig / lean-multisig branch being used for key generation, as there are internal encoding changes in progress.
- **Recursive aggregation (Shariq)**: Finishing up recursive aggregation and swapping out the signature library — main focus for the coming week.

### Post-Quantum Cryptography

- **Q-Day timeline (Unnawut)**: Google released a [paper](https://quantumai.google/static/site-assets/downloads/cryptocurrency-whitepaper.pdf) suggesting quantum computers capable of breaking elliptic curve keys could arrive much sooner. NIST-standardized post-quantum signatures are already available. Short-lived keys (rotated every few minutes) remain relatively safe in the near term; long-lived keys storing significant value are the primary concern.
