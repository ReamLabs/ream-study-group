# Ream Meeting #66 Recap

## Overview

This week's call was a lighter session following a period of travel and conference attendance, with several team members returning from EthCC. Despite the lighter pace, meaningful progress was made across testing infrastructure, network stability, and community outreach. The team also previewed a significant upcoming discussion around the six-month roadmap, covering three candidate focus areas: eth-p2p networking experiments, Goldfish decoupled consensus, and scaling validator counts. That discussion is set to take place in the next pqinterop call.

## Development Progress

### Testing Infrastructure

- **Hive Integration**: An initial PR was opened against the main Hive repository to add Ream as a client. The plan is to expand from there to support multi-client test scenarios, allowing devnet issues to be reproducibly tested in isolation (Kolby).
- **Kurtosis Ethereum Package**: The Kurtosis Ethereum package was forked as a potential path toward scaling up devnet environments for larger validator sets. This is exploratory at this stage (Kolby).
- **Testing Strategy**: Ideas around testing frameworks were gathered from EthCC discussions and are being iterated on internally, with plans to bring proposals forward in the coming days (Shariq).

### devnet-4

- **Work Split & Coordination**: Prior to EthCC, the devnet-4 workload was divided among Kayden, Derek, and Shariq. Given changes during the week away, the team is regrouping on a call to re-align on responsibilities (Kayden, Derek, Shariq).
- **Test Vectors**: Initial investigation into devnet-4 test vectors is underway (Kayden).
- **Justified Checkpoint Fix**: A PR was submitted to fix the update to the state's justified checkpoint used as a source (Kayden).

### Network & Sync Fixes

- **Finalized Checkpoint Route Mismatch**: A PR was merged to handle checkpoint mismatches for anything passed the finalized checkpoint (Derek).
- **Sync Request Consistency**: A PR was submitted to fix a sync issue where block requests by root were decoupled from the peer that provided the checkpoint, causing slowdowns on degraded networks by requesting blocks from peers that may not have had them (Derek).
- **SendQueue Full Fix**: The root cause of the repeated `sendq full` error was identified and fixed. The issue was that Geth under load failed to keep up with gossip sub messages, causing Ream's outbound queue to overflow. The fix leverages the built-in gossip sub slow peer event to detect consistently slow peers and disconnect from them after a grace period (Derek).
- **Peer Dialing Deduplication**: A fix was added to deduplicate outbound connections in the request-response layer, preventing redundant connection attempts to peers already in a connecting state, with added handling for peers with multiple addresses (Derek).

### Community & Outreach

- **EPS (Ethereum Protocol Studies) Presentation**: A presentation on the leanSpec and its surrounding tooling was delivered, running approximately 90 minutes (Unnawut).
- **Roundtable Blog Post**: A [blog post summarizing the recent FORT MODE roundtable discussion](https://hackmd.io/@reamlabs/fort-mode-pq-devnet-ream-view) was completed and published (Unnawut).
- **Study Group Presentation**: Work on the study group presentation on WHIR is ongoing and heavier than anticipated. A commitment was made to present at next week's session (Unnawut).

### Roadmap Discussion (Preview)

- **Three Candidate Directions**: Following EthCC discussions, three potential focus areas for the next six months were identified: (1) eth-p2p networking experimentation, (2) Goldfish decoupled consensus, and (3) stabilizing and significantly scaling the validator count. The priority ordering has not been finalized and will be discussed in tomorrow's call.
- **eth-p2p Architecture**: Clarified that each client would implement its own eth-p2p layer rather than sharing a backend. The motivation is simplicity and performance compared to frameworks like libp2p (Kolby).
